# CQ500 (qure.ai)

- **Tier:** B — reader-disagreement / multi-rater design (per-rater reads released)
- **Modality / anatomy:** Non-contrast head CT (NCCT) / brain + skull
- **Finding type:** Intracranial hemorrhage (+ subtypes IPH/IVH/SDH/EDH/SAH), calvarial fracture, midline shift, mass effect

## Subtlety / perceptibility label
No numeric subtlety field, but the **cleanest genuine multi-reader design in the neuro set**: three radiologists (8, 12, and 20 years' experience) independently read **every** scan, the majority vote is the reference standard, and the **individual per-reader reads are released in the dataset's `reads.csv`**. Inter-reader disagreement is therefore preserved and directly recoverable — a strong difficulty proxy on a region (small ICH, thin SDH, subtle SAH) where reads genuinely diverge.

## Label provenance
3 independent radiologists per scan; both the per-rater reads and the majority-vote consensus are distributed.

## Size
491 scans (batch B1 = 214, B2 = 277), 193,317 slices.

## Access / licensing
Open, free. CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 (non-commercial). http://headctstudy.qure.ai/dataset (mirror: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/crawford/qureai-headct ; Academic Torrents mirror exists). Paper: Chilamkurthy et al., *The Lancet* 2018, https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.05854

## Caveats
- Scan-level (not segmentation) labels.
- Single institution (CARING, New Delhi).
- Non-commercial license.
- Selection enriched for abnormalities (not a screening-prevalence cohort).

## Relevance to detectability
The released per-reader reads make CQ500 a ready-made inter-reader-disagreement testbed for head CT — the neuro analogue of VinDr-CXR / MURA's multi-rater label vectors. Disagreement among three experienced radiologists is the most direct difficulty signal available for small/subtle intracranial hemorrhage.
