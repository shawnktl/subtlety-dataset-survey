# OAI (Osteoarthritis Initiative)

- **Tier:** B — reader-disagreement / multi-rater design (difficulty proxy)
- **Modality / anatomy:** Knee radiograph + 3T MRI / osteoarthritis
- **Finding type:** Osteoarthritis grading (KL, MOAKS/WORMS)

## Subtlety / perceptibility label
No explicit subtlety field on lesions; difficulty is implicit via grading disagreement. MRI BML grade-1 lesions are noted as particularly hard to distinguish from partial-volume artifact — a built-in subtlety phenomenon.

## Label provenance
Two expert readers, KL grading with consensus/senior-reader arbitration. MOAKS/WORMS semi-quantitative scoring also available in OAI sub-studies. Documented inter-reader variability is moderate.

## Size
4,796 participants, longitudinal multi-visit imaging.

## Access / licensing
Open via NIMH/NIA OAI portal (registration + DUA). https://nda.nih.gov/oai/

## Caveats
- No explicit subtlety field on lesions; difficulty is implicit via grading disagreement.

## Relevance to detectability
Multi-rater signal in a non-chest modality (and a non-chest MRI source for the MR gap).
